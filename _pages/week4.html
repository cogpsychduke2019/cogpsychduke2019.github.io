    Learning Objectives:

    Thursday, June 6th - Decision-making (#16/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:

    Resources:

    1. LO1: Continue to build a supportive classroom culture & discuss science communication
      • Class discussion on what it would look like to account for neuroscience or cognitive psychology evidence in the courtroom
      • What do this day's articles imply about neuroscience and policy? Nina Farahany pieces (e.g., consciousness) & how good communication is especially important here.
    2. LO2: Describe the basic foundamental principles of decision-making research
      • Demos on heuristics (1, 2, 3, endowment effect, etc.
      • Basic logical fallacies and information biases that could bias deecision-making
    3. LO3: Summarize and critically analyze academic journal articles
      • How can we apply these findings to everyday life? How does our discussion on sampling factor here? What else would you want to do to build off these studies?
